Syllabus Module Roadmap

Progress systematically through the quantitative layers of preliminary physical frameworks.

01

Module 1: Kinematics

Quantify and graph raw particle motion through space and time. Mathematical treatment of scalar and vector structures, displacement parameters, uniform velocity paths, acceleration intervals, and relative motion profiles in straight lines.

Syllabus Inquiry Focal Points:
  • How can motion be described quantitatively and graphically along straight pathways?
  • How does an observer's perspective change the calculated vector profile of moving objects?
Syllabus Allocation: 25% Time Launch Module Workstation →
02

Module 2: Dynamics

Investigate the root energetic causes underlying changes in standard particle motion states. Formulate models for contact and non-contact interaction pairs, execute multi-force vector resolutions, master Newton's laws of motion, and map energy/momentum tracking transformations.

Syllabus Inquiry Focal Points:
  • How do net forces alter the velocity profiles of macroscopic objects?
  • How is matter transformation regulated by momentum conservation profiles during elastic or inelastic collisions?
Syllabus Allocation: 25% Time Launch Module Workstation →
03

Module 3: Waves and Thermodynamics

Explore structural behaviors of mechanical and electromagnetic wave transport mechanisms alongside thermodynamic models. Solve equations mapping velocity wave fields, reflection/refraction barriers, standing wave geometry, and latent thermal distributions across systems.

Syllabus Inquiry Focal Points:
  • What distinct structural properties differentiate mechanical waves from electromagnetic fields?
  • How does kinetic particle motion scale system thermal capacities and equilibrium states?
Syllabus Allocation: 25% Time Launch Module Workstation →
04

Module 4: Electricity and Magnetism

Analyse stationary electric fields alongside moving charge distributions tracking inside circuit networks. Calculate vector potentials, current flows, potential drops, series-parallel configurations, and fundamental fields operating near magnetic dipoles.

Syllabus Inquiry Focal Points:
  • What operational dynamics establish static electric field gradients between charged matter bounds?
  • How does electrical energy partition across parallel or series resistive circuit configurations?
Syllabus Allocation: 25% Time Launch Module Workstation →
